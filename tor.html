AoL Sub-Committee: Terms of Reference

The Terms of Reference for the Assurance of Learning Sub-Committee of the College Education Quality Committee, established March 2026.

Establishment:March 2026
Authority:Established as a sub-committee of the College Education Quality Committee (CEQC) in accordance with the CEQC Terms of Reference.
Review:Review the Terms of Reference after the first year, then every three years, in alignment with the CEQC review cycle.

Role

The Assurance of Learning (AoL) Sub-Committee supports the College Education Quality Committee in ensuring the continued relevance and effectiveness of programs and courses across the College of Business and Law. AoL is fundamentally about education quality: it provides the College with a systematic, evidence-based process for evaluating whether students are achieving the intended learning outcomes of their programs and for using that evidence to drive continuous improvement in curriculum design and delivery.

AoL operates through a continuous improvement cycle in which learning goals and objectives are defined at the program level, embedded in course-level assignments, measured systematically, and the resulting data is used to inform curriculum review and enhancement. The Sub-Committee provides direction and oversight for this cycle across the College, including activities that underpin the College's accreditation obligations under AACSB International and EQUIS standards.

The Sub-Committee takes a programmatic approach to curriculum review and enhancement, driven by Program Learning Outcome (PLO) data and informed by stakeholder engagement. It ensures that closing-the-loop actions are identified, implemented and monitored, and that AoL findings contribute meaningfully to improvements in teaching and learning quality across the College.

Functions

The Assurance of Learning Sub-Committee will:

  1. Oversee the development, implementation and continuous improvement of AoL processes across the College, ensuring they are comprehensive, robust and sustainable.
  2. Review AoL data, including assignment outcomes mapped to Program Learning Outcomes, and evaluate program performance to identify emerging risks and opportunities for improvement.
  3. Review and approve proposals for closing-the-loop actions, including the identification of synergies across the program portfolio, and monitor their implementation and effectiveness.
  4. Conduct regular reviews of skills development maps and curriculum alignment for all programs within the AoL framework, ensuring PLOs remain current and appropriately assessed.
  5. Ensure AoL processes and documentation meet the requirements of relevant accreditation bodies, including AACSB and EQUIS, and support the College's accreditation and reaccreditation activities.
  6. Engage internal and external stakeholders, including industry and students, to ensure the continued relevance of the College's learning outcomes framework.
  7. Report to the College Education Quality Committee on AoL activities, findings and recommended curriculum or course amendments.

Reporting Line

The Sub-Committee reports to the College Education Quality Committee. It may provide additional reports to the College Executive as required.

Frequency of Meetings

The Sub-Committee will meet four times per year, twice per semester, for one hour. Additional meetings may be called by the Chair if required to address urgent matters.

Quorum

Half the existing membership, including the Chair or a designated delegate (ignoring fractions), plus one.

Membership

  • Dean, Academic (Chair)
  • Academic/Portfolio Lead (representative)
  • Undergraduate Program Director/s (representative)
  • Postgraduate Program Director/s (representative)
  • AoL Director (pending workload arrangement; title to be confirmed)
  • Associate Director, Academic and Program Services
  • Accreditation and Quality Assurance Manager
  • Program Quality Assurance Officer
  • Work Integrated Learning representative

Other members may be invited by the Chair to attend and assist the Sub-Committee to fulfil its functions. This includes representatives from other Schools within the College (including the School of Law), central units such as the Teaching and Learning Institute, or the Student Engagement Unit.
