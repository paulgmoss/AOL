AoL in Practice: Examples from the Field

External Exemplar

How data and digital learning tools can refine teaching

Times Higher Education Campus | Published externally

This account from the Times Higher Education Campus resource describes how one academic used learning data and digital tools, including outcome-level data generated through an LMS, to identify where students were struggling and to make targeted adjustments to their teaching. The case illustrates several principles that are central to the AoL approach being implemented here.

The author describes the experience of moving from grade-based monitoring, where a reasonable pass rate gave a false sense of security, to outcome-level data that revealed specific areas where students were consistently underperforming despite passing overall. This is precisely the limitation of grade-as-proxy that the Business School's AoL strategy is designed to address. The shift required both a technical change in how assignment data was captured and a conceptual shift in what the academic was looking for when reviewing results.

Of particular relevance is the account of how having real-time cohort-level data changed the academic's ability to respond during the semester, rather than only after it. Seeing that a specific outcome was not being well attained mid-course created the opportunity to adjust tutorial focus, add formative checkpoints, and prompt students to revisit foundational material before the final assignment. The feedback loop operated within a single teaching period rather than across cycles.

The account is also honest about the initial time investment involved in setting up the system correctly and the importance of institutional support in making that investment manageable. This mirrors the support model TLI is providing across the College in 2026.

Key takeaways relevant to AoL at the College

  • Grade-based monitoring can mask specific outcome-level gaps even when overall results look acceptable
  • Real-time cohort data enables in-semester response, not just end-of-cycle reflection
  • The initial setup investment is manageable with the right support and pays dividends across subsequent offerings
  • The data changes not just what you know about student performance but how you think about course design
