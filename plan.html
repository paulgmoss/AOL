College Plan 2026

The 2026 Staged Implementation Plan

The phased approach to AoL implementation across the College in 2026, including scope, timeline, and the opt-in pathway for staff ready to engage now.

The overarching goal is clear: AoL should encompass all courses across the College as part of our commitment to education quality. For 2026, a staged approach has been designed that builds toward this goal progressively, starting with a targeted set of courses and expanding from 2027 with a clear roster mechanism to ensure nothing falls through the gaps.

The 2026 Staged Approach

S1

Semester 1 2026 Final Assignment Focus

For courses in scope in Semester 1, the focus is on the final assignment only. Course coordinators are not being asked to change their assignments. TLI will work one-on-one with each course coordinator to confirm which CLOs the final assignment captures and to ensure these are correctly reflected in the Canvas rubric. For most courses this will take no more than 15 minutes.

S2

Semester 2 2026 Extended to Two Assignments

From Semester 2, AoL data will be collected from two major assignment tasks per course. This gives richer and more reliable data on how CLOs are being achieved across a program of study, while remaining manageable. The focus is on substantive, summative tasks that provide a meaningful picture of student learning outcomes.

27

From 2027 Expanded Roster and Full Coverage

A roster will be developed during 2026 for transitioning additional courses into the AoL framework from 2027. This provides a clear, monitored mechanism to build toward comprehensive coverage across the College over time, ensuring progress is tracked and no program is left behind.

Which Courses Are in Scope for 2026

The 2026 scope focuses on first-year courses within the eight programs designated for AACSB reporting, with a minimum of three courses per program selected to ensure adequate coverage of Program Learning Outcomes. Priority is given to courses offered in both semesters, so that there is an opportunity to close the loop within the year itself, demonstrating improvement within a single reporting cycle.

Primary Scope

AACSB Reporting Programs

Eight programs designated for AACSB reporting, one undergraduate and one postgraduate per school. First-year courses within these programs are the priority, with a minimum of three courses per program selected.

Secondary Scope

All Other Programs

Data will be collected across all programs, with formal AACSB reporting focused on the designated programs. This builds the broader evidence base the College needs for long-term quality assurance.

Opt-In Opportunity

Alongside the staged rollout, there is an open opt-in opportunity for any course coordinator who already sees the value in this work and has the capacity to engage now. If you are ready to participate in AoL data collection regardless of whether your course is in the primary 2026 scope, we would welcome your involvement. Contact TLI to discuss how to get started.

Note
Courses already participating in AoL will be generating the evidence required for Annual Program Monitoring and University Program Reviews as a natural byproduct of their marking practice, rather than needing to produce it through a separate process when those reviews fall due.
