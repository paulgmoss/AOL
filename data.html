Data and Reporting

From Rubric to Report: How AoL Data Is Used

How the data you generate through careful rubric design and Canvas setup travels through the system to inform programme-level reporting and AACSB accreditation evidence.

Understanding how your rubric marking contributes to programme-level reporting, and ultimately to AACSB accreditation, helps make the AoL process feel purposeful rather than procedural. This page traces that journey from the moment you apply a rubric criterion to a student submission, through to the College's annual reporting cycle. It also includes a live view of where the College currently sits in the reporting cycle, so staff can see the process in action.

How Data Flows Through the System

1

Rubric marking: a straightforward judgement call

When marking a student submission, the marker makes a judgement about how each rubric criterion has been met. Where criteria are mapped to CLOs, this marking process also captures outcome attainment. It is not automated: the marker determines how the criterion score, or the aggregated score across multiple criteria mapped to the same CLO, translates to an Exceeds, Meets, or Does Not Meet outcome level. In practice this is a quick and intuitive call once the rubric is set up correctly.

Part B of the Canvas How-To Guide covers the marking process in detail, across all three rubric configurations.

2

CLO attainment recorded and aggregated in Canvas

Once the marker has made their outcome-level judgement, Canvas records the CLO attainment for that student. Where a CLO is assessed across multiple assignments, Canvas aggregates the results using the calculation method configured for that outcome (weighted average by default). The Learning Mastery Pathway view shows each student their attainment level for every CLO, and shows the course coordinator the same data at cohort level in real time.

3

CLO data mapped to PLOs

Each CLO has already been mapped to one or more Programme Learning Outcomes as part of the curriculum architecture. CLO attainment data flows upward through this mapping to produce a PLO attainment profile for each program, with later-year CLOs carrying greater weight to reflect their stronger correlation with graduate-level capability.

4

Programme team formalises the data

At the end of each teaching period, the program team compiles and reviews the PLO attainment data across all participating courses. This is not about generating new mappings; those are already in place. It is about bringing the data together, reviewing what it shows, and preparing it for committee consideration. Two programs per school are designated as primary AACSB reporting programs, one undergraduate and one postgraduate, with data collected across all programs.

5

University-wide quality processes

The Curriculum Quality Assurance Procedure requires Course Coordinators to monitor learning analytics, student performance data, and alignment with program learning outcomes every time a course is delivered. That course-level monitoring feeds into Annual Program Monitoring which in turn informs University Program Reviews. Courses participating in AoL generate exactly this evidence as a natural part of their marking practice, rather than needing to produce it through a separate process later.

6

Closing the loop

Where PLO attainment falls below expectation, the AoL Committee traces the result back through the CLO data to identify which courses and outcomes are contributing to the gap. Targeted interventions are designed and implemented, and their impact is measured in the following data collection cycle. This closing-the-loop evidence is what AACSB peer review teams look for: not perfect results, but a demonstrable culture of using evidence to improve.

These results came from a real AoL cycle in which CLO-level analysis revealed that poor attainment on PLO 1 was concentrated in specific courses and specific outcomes, not distributed evenly across the programme. Interventions included restructuring assignment timing so formative tasks preceded summative ones for underperforming CLOs, redesigning rubric criteria to better capture the intended outcomes, and in one case a full course redesign. The improvement across a single cycle demonstrates that outcome-level precision, rather than grade-based monitoring, is what makes targeted intervention possible.

The AoL Improvement Cycle

The following six-stage cycle describes the full AoL process from preparation through to improvement. It outlines who is responsible at each stage and what actions are taken. Between the end-of-semester marking period and the AoL Sub-Committee review, the Program Team, including course lecturers and tutors, meet to discuss the data and contribute to the draft program report. The report is then finalised and submitted to the Sub-Committee for review.

Stage 1

Prepare

The Dean (portfolio lead) informs Course Coordinators of required data collection and any closing-the-loop actions from the previous cycle. Course Coordinators review assignments to ensure they enable students to achieve CLOs and PLOs.

Stage 2

Implement

The Program Team updates assignments or teaching methods based on previous closing-the-loop actions. Course Coordinators assess students against PLOs and CLOs, completing marking in Canvas.

Stage 3

Analyse

The Assessment Review Committee undertakes quality assurance on assessment outcomes. The Program Quality Assurance Officer generates a draft program report with an initial summary and analysis of the Canvas data.

Stage 4

Review

Program Directors, Course Coordinators and the broader Program Team review the draft report, offer insights, and propose closing-the-loop actions. The AoL Working Group reviews reports before submission to the College Education Quality Committee.

Stage 5

Approve

The College Education Quality Committee approves the final reports with action items and submits to College Executive for endorsement. College Executive endorses all program reports at the end of each year.

Stage 6

Improve

The Program Quality Assurance Officer advises the Program Team of required closing-the-loop actions. Program Directors and Course Coordinators plan implementation of action items in conjunction with the teaching team.

Note
For details on the governance structure overseeing this cycle, including membership, functions and meeting frequency, see the AoL Sub-Committee Terms of Reference.

What happens with the data and when you will hear back

End of Semester 1 marking period: The program team compiles and formalises PLO attainment data across all participating courses, drawing on the CLO-to-PLO mappings already in place. This data is prepared for AoL Committee review.

AoL Committee review: The committee meets to review PLO attainment results, identify any patterns of concern, and propose targeted interventions where needed.

Results communicated back to course coordinators: Each course coordinator whose data has been included in the cycle will receive a summary of findings relevant to their course, including where CLO attainment was strong, where gaps were identified, and any recommended actions. This is a commitment, not an aspiration. The data belongs to the teaching team and will be shared with them.

Semester 2 planning: Findings from Semester 1 inform the design of the Semester 2 data collection cycle, which extends to two major assignments per course.

Key
One of the lessons from previous AoL processes is that data collected but never communicated back to Teaching Academics serves no one. The College is committed to closing that loop at the course level, not just the programme level. If you participate in AoL data collection in 2026, you will receive findings relevant to your course.

A Note on the Transition Period

Adelaide University brings together the curriculum architectures of the University of Adelaide and the University of South Australia. During 2026, the AoL reporting framework is being designed to draw on the best of both approaches while building toward a unified system. This means some processes and reporting formats will evolve across the year. The School will communicate any changes clearly as they arise. The core requirement, that CLOs are mapped to rubric criteria in Canvas, that attainment data is captured at outcome level, and that this data informs programme-level review, remains consistent throughout.
