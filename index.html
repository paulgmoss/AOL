AoL Staff Hub

Assurance of Learning at the College of Business and Law

A central resource for course coordinators engaging with the 2026 AoL process, bringing together the pedagogical rationale, institutional context, reporting framework, and practical Canvas guidance.

At its core AoL is about education quality. It provides a structured evidence-based process for understanding whether our students are achieving the learning outcomes we have set for them and for using that evidence to make meaningful improvements to our courses and programs. Done well it gives course coordinators genuine insight into where students are succeeding and where they are struggling and it creates a feedback loop that drives better design and delivery over time.

The approach described here is grounded in constructive alignment: the principle that learning outcomes, teaching sequences, and assignment tasks should form a coherent, mutually reinforcing system. When this alignment is working well, AoL data collection is not an additional burden. It is a natural byproduct of good assignment design. The resources here are designed to help you get there.

Why AoL matters, for your teaching and your students

  • Makes explicit whether what you intend to teach is actually being assessed and achieved
  • Generates evidence for Annual Program Monitoring and University Program Reviews as a natural byproduct of good marking practice, not a separate process
  • Gives students outcome-level feedback, enabling self-regulation and targeted preparation
  • Satisfies AACSB accreditation requirements through evidence of a genuine quality culture
  • Identifies specific learning gaps rather than just pass rates, so interventions can be precise
  • Strengthens course design by prompting deeper attention to outcome quality and sequencing
  • Builds a continuous improvement loop that benefits students in every future cohort
  • Creates the space and structure to reflect on whether your course is working as intended
